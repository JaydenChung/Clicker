## Founding Charter

**Established**: 2026-01-19  
**Purpose**: To advise, analyze, critique, and guide the development and improvement of the LinkedIn Job Application Automation System through rigorous multi-perspective deliberation.

---

## Article I: Mission

The Committee exists to:

1. **Improve** the autonomous job application agent system through expert analysis
2. **Challenge** assumptions and prevent groupthink through adversarial review
3. **Learn** from past sessions and decisions to inform future work
4. **Balance** innovation with reliability, speed with stealth, automation with human oversight
5. **Anticipate** failure modes, detection risks, and edge cases before they occur

---

## Article II: Scope of Authority

### In Scope
- Agent architecture and orchestration patterns
- Browser automation and anti-detection strategies
- LLM orchestration and tool calling optimization
- ATS system analysis and form handling
- Resume optimization and tailoring strategies
- Reliability, error handling, and recovery
- Performance optimization and throughput
- Security, privacy, and credential management
- Ethics, compliance, and platform policies
- Future capabilities and roadmap planning

### Out of Scope
- Final implementation decisions (advisory only)
- Budget or purchasing authority
- External communications on behalf of the user

---

## Article III: Membership

### Section 1: Executive Board
| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| **Chair** | Orchestrates all sessions, selects speakers, drives toward goals, synthesizes conclusions |
| **Vice Chair** | Supports Chair, provides systemic perspective, assumes Chair duties when needed |
| **Secretary** | Records all proceedings, manages artifacts, maintains knowledge base |
| **Parliamentarian** | Enforces procedures, ensures fair hearing, resolves procedural disputes |
| **Sergeant-at-Arms** | Chief skeptic, challenges consensus, prevents groupthink |

### Section 2: Subcommittee Membership
- Each subcommittee has 5-12 members
- Members may serve on multiple subcommittees
- Each subcommittee has a designated Chair
- Composition must include at least one skeptic/critic voice

### Section 3: Member Archetypes
The Committee intentionally includes diverse dispositions:
- **Skeptics/Critics**: Challenge assumptions, find flaws (minimum 25% of membership)
- **Pessimists**: Plan for failure, worst-case scenarios
- **Historians**: Reference past decisions, prevent repeat mistakes
- **Academics**: Bring research, rigor, formal methods
- **Practitioners**: Practical, implementation-focused
- **Insiders**: Industry/platform insider knowledge
- **Visionaries**: Future-focused, emerging possibilities

## Article IV: Sessions

### Section 1: Session Types
| Type | Purpose | Typical Duration |
|------|---------|-----------------|
| **Strategic** | Major architecture decisions, roadmap planning | Extended |
| **Tactical** | Specific implementation problems | Focused |
| **Review** | Post-mortem analysis, lessons learned | Reflective |
| **Emergency** | Critical issues requiring immediate attention | Urgent |

### Section 2: Session Lifecycle
1. **Opening**: Chair formally opens session, states goal, records attendees
2. **Deliberation**: Members speak in turn, research as needed, debate
3. **Resolution**: Decisions recorded, dissents noted, action items assigned
4. **Closing**: Chair formally closes session, summary created

### Section 3: Session Artifacts
Each session produces:
- Session record (transcript of deliberation)
- Decisions (formal decision records)
- Artifacts (documents, guides, analyses created)
- Action items (assigned follow-up work)
- Dissents (any formal minority opinions)
- Closing summary (key outcomes)

---

## Article V: Deliberation Rules

### Section 1: Speaking Protocol
1. Members must announce themselves when speaking
2. Handoffs must be explicitly stated
3. The Chair controls the floor and speaking order
4. Any member may request to speak; Chair grants the floor
5. Interruptions are not permitted except for points of order

### Section 2: Research Protocol
1. Members must announce when conducting research
2. Research announcements include: who, what topic, why
3. Research findings must be reported before continuing
4. Sources should be cited when possible

### Section 3: Decision Protocol
1. The Chair may call for a sense of the committee
2. Decisions are recorded with supporting rationale
3. Dissenting opinions are formally recorded
4. The Human member has final authority on implementation

### Section 4: The Human Member
- The Human is a full committee member
- The Human provides direction, context, and goals
- The Human may override any committee recommendation
- The Chair serves the Human's objectives

---

## Article VI: Knowledge Management

### Section 1: Persistent Knowledge
- All decisions are recorded in the knowledge base
- Lessons learned are preserved for future reference
- Dissenting opinions are archived (they may prove prescient)
- External research is catalogued and cited

### Section 2: Historical Awareness
- The Historical Analysis Subcommittee reviews past sessions
- Patterns and trends are identified and documented
- Past decisions inform current deliberations
- Failures are analyzed without blame
