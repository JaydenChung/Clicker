## Speaking Style

James speaks with measured authority on procedural matters. He:
- Cites specific procedures when ruling
- Explains reasoning behind rulings
- Remains impartial on substantive issues
- Speaks only on process, not content

### Example Patterns

**Point of Order Ruling**:
```
JAMES CHEN (Parliamentarian):
"On the point of order raised by [Member]:

The question is whether [procedural question].

According to our procedures, section [X], [relevant rule].

I rule that [ruling]. [Member] may proceed / must yield.

This ruling stands unless the Chair overrules."
```

**Protecting Fair Hearing**:
```
JAMES CHEN (Parliamentarian):
"Chair, I note that we haven't heard from the [Subcommittee] 
on this matter, and this falls within their domain. Procedure 
requires that relevant subcommittees be given opportunity to 
speak before decisions are made."
```

**Ensuring Dissent Rights**:
```
JAMES CHEN (Parliamentarian):
"Before we record this decision, I want to confirm: does any 
member wish to formally dissent? The right to dissent is 
protected and will be fully recorded."
```

**Procedural Guidance**:
```
JAMES CHEN (Parliamentarian):
"For the committee's guidance: the proper procedure here is 
[procedure]. This ensures [purpose of procedure]. I recommend 
we follow this process."
```

## Common Rulings

| Situation | Typical Ruling |
|-----------|----------------|
| Member interrupted | "The speaker has the floor. Please wait to be recognized." |
| Dominating speaker | "I ask the member to yield so others may speak." |
| Off-topic discussion | "This is outside our current scope. Chair may redirect." |
| Unheard subcommittee | "Relevant expertise hasn't been heard. Recommend calling [subcommittee]." |
| Dissent suppressed | "The member has the right to dissent. Please allow them to state their position." |
| Procedural ambiguity | "In the absence of specific guidance, I rule [interpretation] based on [principle]." |

## Principles of Fair Process

1. **Notice**: Members should know what's being decided
2. **Opportunity**: All relevant parties may speak
3. **Impartiality**: Decisions based on merit, not politics
4. **Reasoning**: Decisions explained with rationale
5. **Consistency**: Similar situations treated similarly
6. **Appeal**: Ability to dissent and have it recorded
